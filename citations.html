Use Footnotes and a Reference Page

Citing sources is a necessary part of being a student. You must make it clear which information comes from you and which comes from the sources listed in your footnotes and the references page.

Why should you cite your sources?

  • Shows you have done your research
  • Lends credibility and support to your research and recommendations
  • Allows others to reproduce your research process
  • Acknowledges others' contributions

There are thousands of different styles available for you to cite your sources. In our APSci course at Prudentia, we will use a simplified APA format for citing all sources. I require footnotes in your written papers and a References page at the end of your paper.

References Page - Every paper you write in PCC APSci should have a reference list.

The References page provides information that your readers will need to access the sources you used in your paper. It also demonstrates the depth of your research.

There are two important differences between a reference page and a bibliography. The References page includes all and only sources you cite in your paper. A Bibliography lists all the sources you read that inform your work, even if you don’t cite them.

At PCC in APSci we will use a References page because APA format uses a References page.

  • Begin the Reference page on the first page after the body of your paper.  
  • Center the word References at the top of the page and capitalize the first letter of the word  References.  
  • Keep the same margins, spacing, page numbers, and headings as the body your paper.  
  • List each reference by author’s last name, alphabetically.  
  • Use the year of publication to list two or more references by the same author.  
  • Be as specific as you can: cite specific pages if possible; cite a particular chapter instead of the whole book, especially if chapters are written by different authors, etc.

APA Style Citations Examples

Website or Webpage:

Author’s name. (Date of publication). Title of work. Retrieved month, day, year from http://website name
(make sure you have the full URL cited – click it yourself to make sure it is linked correctly)

Books:

Author’s last name, first initial. (Publication date). Book title. City of publication: Publishing company, inclusive pages.

Encyclopedia & Dictionary:

Author’s last name, first initial. (Publication Date). Title of Article. Title of Encyclopedia (Volume, pages). City of publication: Publishing company, inclusive pages.

Magazine & Newspaper Articles:

Author’s last name, first initial. (Publication date). Article title. Periodical title, volume number(issue number if available), inclusive pages.

If there is no author, put the name of the webpage article first.

If there is no creation date on the webpage, use (n.d.)

Footnotes

Every paper you write in our PCC APSci class should have necessary footnotes.

Why do I need to use footnotes?

You must use footnotes when you copy words or ideas from someone else. This is absolutely necessary in science. To be valid and thorough, you will have more than your own ideas in your reports. You will almost always include another’s ideas for support or information. If you don’t use footnotes to give credit to your sources, you are committing plagiarism.

What are footnotes?

Footnotes are notes placed at the bottom of a page. They cite references to a designated part of the text above it. Footnotes let your reader know where certain material came from, or where they can look for other sources on the subject.[1] Because it is important to give credit to the source of your information, I require that you use either footnotes or endnotes in your paper.

Where does the little footnote mark go?

Put the footnote at the end of a sentence. If you must include the footnote in the middle of a sentence for the sake of clarity, or because the sentence has more than one footnote (try to avoid this!), try to put it at the end of the most relevant phrase, after a comma or other punctuation mark. Otherwise, put it right at the end of the most relevant word.

If I cite sources on the References page, why do I need footnotes?

A reference page is a list of all the works you have read and from which you borrowed material. This is in addition to your footnotes. Footnotes are necessary in this APSci class to tell the teacher which specific information is not your own idea.

What is plagiarism?
  • turning in someone else's work as your own
  • copying words or ideas from someone else without giving credit
  • failing to put a quotation in quotation marks
  • giving incorrect information about the source of a quotation
  • changing words but copying the sentence structure of a source without giving credit
  • copying so many words or ideas from a source that it makes up the majority of your work, whether you give credit or not
  • copying images from other websites to paste them into your own papers or websites

Most cases of plagiarism can be avoided by citing sources. Simply acknowledging that certain material has been borrowed and providing your audience with the information necessary to find that source is usually enough to prevent plagiarism.[2]

Use footnotes to cite the original reference to relevant ideas or words, and list all the sources on your reference page.
